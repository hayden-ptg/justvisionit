Tradeshow Printing FAQ

Tradeshow Printing Details

We can be a one stop shop for your trade show needs. We can print on the fabric, supply hardware for nearly any hardware supplier, and make promo products such as the shirts and pillows you see above! Large format printing for trade show displays is one of our top specialties.

What brands of fixtures and fittings do we work with?

We are a REX Frame dealer and Matrix frame dealer for all aluminum extrusion frames. We also carry a full line of Modular SEG hardware as well. We can get any range of fixtures and fittings from the some of the biggest names in Trade Show Display industry.  We have a great relationship with all of them, and in the unlikely situation we don’t have a particular display type in stock, we can order it in and will receive it fast.

What do we need from you?

  • Finished Artwork. (Formats detailed in our “For you, the Printer” guidelines)
  • Deadline for Delivery.
  • Any specific client instructions.
  • A list of fixtures/fittings needed with your order.

Can we use any fonts or colors?

Sure you can.  The amount of color or the color itself will be printed just like what is in your artwork.  

What resolutions should artwork be displayed in?

You want your digital files to be at actual product size and 100dpi or higher for the best turn out – we print quality work as we are 1 of only a few G7 certified dye sublimation printers in the entire USA!

What's the turn around time?

On standard products it is 3 to 7 business days from the time your artwork is approved. Custom projects may require extra time.

What are your finishing services?

  • Sewn Pole Pockets
  • Silicone Beading
  • Hemmed Edges
  • Grommets (several styles)
  • Wind Slits
  • Velcro
  • Contour Cutting
  • Stretched to Fit
  • Full Custom Edging as Needed

What are your hardware systems?

  • Orbus (full Orbus 365 line-up; Origin, Sign Pro Systems, Nimlok)
  • REX Frame
  • TestRite
  • Nomadic
  • SEG Framing Systems
  • AGAM (made in USA)

What is custom fabrication?

Creating trade show displays is not just printing.   Its cutting, sewing, attaching ropes/Velcro/ties where necessary, sourcing the fixtures and fittings to display the banners, custom welding fabrication for full custom booth framing. It’s a whole production process that can take the average printer outside their comfort zone.
